How to contact the IOCCC

✓

WAIT!!! - Before you send that email

If you are trying to:

  • Fix a winning IOCCC entry - See our FAQ on “fixing entries”
  • Update winning author information - See our FAQ on “fixing author information”
  • Correct a website problem - See our FAQ on “fixing the website” or our FAQ on “fix URL”

Instead of sending us email, PLEASE consider using the GitHub pull request process against the master branch of the ioccc-src/winner repo. See the FAQ on “submitting a pull request”.

If you are trying to:

  • Report a bug in an IOCCC winning entry - See our FAQ on “reporting an entry bug”
  • Report an IOCCC website problem - See our FAQ on “reporting a website problem”
  • Report a broken or wrong web link - See our FAQ on “reporting a broken link”

In general, unless you have a fix, if you want to report something, you should instead first look at the IOCCC issues to see if the problem has already been reported. If it has been reported, feel free to add a comment to the issue. Otherwise, if you do not see the same issue reported, then feel free to open a new IOCCC issue.

If you are trying to:

  • Comment or make a suggestion on IOCCC rules - See our FAQ on “rules feedback”
  • Comment or make a suggestion on IOCCC guidelines - See our FAQ on “guidelines feedback”
  • Comment or make a suggestion on IOCCC tools - See our FAQ on “tools feedback”

Those FAQ entries will help guide you on how to make public comments and suggestion in the relevant places.

See the above FAQ entries for more details.

Try Mastodon

Please follow the IOCCC on Mastodon

We use Mastodon to make minor announcements, in addition announcing major events such as the opening of a new IOCCC, or who won.

See the FAQ on “Mastodon” for more information on Mastodon.

If you really need to send email the IOCCC judges

Did you look at the above section?

If you must contact the IOCCC judges by email, then please send email to: q.YYYY@ioccc.org

IMPORTANT: The YYYY in the above email address MUST be changed to the current 4 digit year!

You MUST include the following two words (separated by a space) in the subject of your email, otherwise the email will be rejected:

    IOCCC YYYY

IMPORTANT: The YYYY in the above email address MUST be changed to the current 4 digit year!

PLEASE NOTE: Do NOT immediately proceed nor follow the two words with punctuation!

The subject line for an email should not contain something such as IOCCC YYYY. or Re:IOCCC YYYY because there are non-whitespace characters right next to the 2 words. Instead, try something like I have an IOCCC YYYY question. or Re: IOCCC YYYY or just IOCCC YYYY on the subject line. And of course, replace YYYY in these examples with the current 4-digit year.
